# The Light

At the top of the tower the world was very simple. There was the lamp, and
there was the dark, and between them there was a man whose whole life had been
an argument in favour of the lamp.

He struck the match. The wick caught. And the beam went out across the water —
nineteen miles of borrowed daylight — and found, at the edge of its reach, the
thing that had been coming, and held it there, pinned and ordinary, in the
plain and patient light.
