# The Fog

The fog came the way grief comes — not all at once, but in a slow erasure of
everything you had used to steer by. First the far buoy. Then the near one.
Then the horizon itself, folded up and put away.

Aldous climbed the ninety-two steps he had climbed a hundred thousand times,
and with each one the lamp grew louder above him, its great slow turning like
the breath of something patient and enormous. He did not hurry. Hurry was for
men who believed the light could be lost. He knew better: the light was never
lost. Only keepers were.
